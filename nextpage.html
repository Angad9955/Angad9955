A to Z information on Ram Mandir

The construction of the Ram Mandir, also known as the Ram Janmabhoomi Mandir, in Ayodhya, India, is a significant and complex historical and religious event. Below is a comprehensive overview of the Ram Mandir, covering various aspects:

Historical Background:

Ayodhya: Ayodhya is a city in the northern Indian state of Uttar Pradesh, believed to be the birthplace of Lord Rama.

Ram Janmabhoomi: Ram Janmabhoomi is considered the birthplace of Lord Rama, a revered figure in Hinduism.

Babri Masjid: The Babri Masjid was a mosque built in Ayodhya during the Mughal era in the 16th century.

Disput and Legal Battles

Babri Masjid-Ram Janmabhoomi Dispute: The Babri Masjid-Ram Janmabhoomi dispute was a longstanding issue, with competing claims over the site.

Demolition (1992): On December 6, 1992, the Babri Masjid was demolished by a large mob, leading to communal tensions.

Legal Proceedings: Legal battles followed, with various courts addressing the ownership and status of the site.

Supreme Court Verdict (2019):

Historic Verdict: In November 2019, the Supreme Court of India delivered a historic verdict. The court ruled in favor of the construction of a Ram Mandir at the disputed site.

Land Allotment: The court also allotted an alternative plot to the Muslim community for the construction of a mosque.

Bhoomi Pujan and Construction:

Bhoomi Pujan (August 2020): On August 5, 2020, a grand ceremony marked the beginning of the construction of the Ram Mandir. Prime Minister Narendra Modi, religious leaders, and dignitaries attended the event.
